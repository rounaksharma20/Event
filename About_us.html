Aligned to the Bologna system, universities in the EU and EEA countries use the European Credit Transfer and Accumulation System (ECTS) as the main grading system in universities, as well as a way to keep track of credit transfers for students coming from these countries. So, how many credits for a Bachelor's or Master's degree in Europe?
In ECTS, a full study year normally consists of 60 credits, so completing a Bachelor’s degree would require earning 180 credits and a Master’s degree would require 120 credit points.
